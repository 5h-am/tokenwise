# Economics

The value of a converted lead depends on what Credex earns from a credit purchase. For this model, I am assuming an average first purchase value of $2,400 and a 25% gross margin to Credex, so one closed customer is worth about $600 in gross profit before support time. If the real Credex margin is higher or the average purchase is larger, the funnel gets much more attractive. Sham should replace these assumptions with actual Credex numbers before submission if they are available.

| Channel | Cash CAC | Real cost | Notes |
| --- | ---: | --- | --- |
| Existing Credex customer/lead list | $0 | Founder time | Best channel because users are already AI-credit buyers. |
| Hacker News Show HN | $0 | One strong post plus comment time | Spiky, but a good fit for technical founders. |
| Indie Hackers / Ramen Club / Slack groups | $0 | Manual posting and replies | Works only if the post shows the tool, not a pitch. |
| Cold DMs to founders and CTOs | $0 | Slow manual research | Best for learning and first consultations. |
| SEO/content | $0 cash | Too slow for 30 days | Useful later, weak for launch week. |

Funnel assumptions for a focused launch:

| Step | Monthly count | Conversion |
| --- | ---: | ---: |
| Visitors | 1,500 | - |
| Completed audits | 600 | 40% of visitors |
| High-savings audits over $500/mo | 90 | 15% of completions |
| Email leads from high-savings users | 18 | 20% of high-savings audits |
| Consultations booked | 5 | 25% of leads |
| Credit purchases | 3 | 60% of consultations |

At $2,400 average first purchase value, 3 purchases/month is $7,200 in monthly sales from this channel. At the assumed 25% gross margin, that is $1,800/month in gross profit. Since the cash CAC is near zero, the early question is not ad efficiency. It is whether the product reliably produces qualified, high-intent consultations.

To reach $1M ARR, the business needs about $83,333 MRR. If the average monthly revenue equivalent per customer is $2,400, that means about 35 new deals per month. With a 60% consultation-to-purchase close rate, 35 deals require 58 consultations. With a 25% lead-to-consultation rate, 58 consultations require 232 qualified leads. With a 20% email capture rate from high-savings users, 232 leads require 1,160 high-savings audits. If only 15% of completed audits are high-savings, that requires about 7,733 completed audits per month.

That is plausible in 18 months only if the unfair Credex channel works and referrals compound. It is not plausible from manual posting alone. The product would need distribution through Credex's credit relationships, partner newsletters, founder communities, and maybe a lightweight embedded audit that accelerators or startup finance advisors can share. The number to watch is not traffic; it is high-savings completed audits. If those do not show up, the lead quality will never support the revenue target.
