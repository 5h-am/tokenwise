# Go-to-market

## Who it is for

The person I picture is a technical founder, CTO, VP Engineering, or ops lead at a small company (roughly 5–100 people) where AI tools spread faster than any real buying process. They are not running formal procurement. They are watching Cursor, Copilot, ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, v0, and API line items land on different cards and asking whether the team is paying twice for the same kind of work.

They do not wake up wanting “an audit product.” They search when something hurts: renewal time, a messy statement, or five people on five separate AI subscriptions. Queries look like team pricing for Cursor vs Copilot, whether ChatGPT Team is worth it, Claude seat minimums, OpenAI API cost, or how to cut AI tool spend at a startup. Those searches are urgent and specific, not generic curiosity.

## Where they hang out

They show up in builder-heavy corners of the internet: **r/startups**, **r/SaaS**, Indie Hackers, small founder Slacks, Ramen-style communities, people shipping on Vercel/Next, and **Hacker News**. The message that works is practical, not flashy — something like: I built a free AI spend audit for small teams because it is too easy to pay for Cursor, Copilot, Claude, and ChatGPT at the same time. On HN I would post a **Show HN** on a weekday morning (US Pacific), stay in the thread, and answer with real numbers from the audit engine instead of marketing language.

## What I already did

Before I froze the product shape, I sent a **Google Form** through **WhatsApp** (friends, uni, people who actually build) and through **Reddit** in subs where founders and developers actually post. That costs almost nothing. You still get “marketing” out of it: the form explains the problem in your words and carries a link, so people see the idea before a polished launch. When someone fills even a short form, they often feel they **helped shape** what ships next. That feeling is hard to buy with ads, and it tends to come back as early testers, blunt feedback, and shares when you launch.

## What I would do next with no ad budget

**One:** A Show HN post and an Indie Hackers post, each with a real screenshot from a sample audit so people see output, not a landing promise.

**Two:** Personally message founders or engineering managers who recently posted about AI tools, hiring, or infra spend. Keep the ask tiny: free audit, two minutes, no billing upload.

**Three:** Short threads or posts comparing real stacks (for example Cursor + Copilot on a three-person team, or ChatGPT Team + Claude for a content-heavy team). Useful content pulls the right readers.

**Four:** Ask people who got a strong audit if they know **one** other founder with a lot of AI seats. Warm intros beat cold reach.

**Credex:** Teams that already bought discounted credits are a strong channel if that fits the business. They already care about AI spend. A simple launch email can be direct: free stack audit before the next renewal.

## Week one (example targets)

Targets should stay modest and measurable — for example: **75** completed audits, **20** emails captured on the results side, **8** audits above a meaningful savings bar, and a small number of serious follow-ups if you run a human sales step. If traffic shows up but almost nobody leaves an email, the results page is probably not earning trust yet — fix that before chasing more traffic.
