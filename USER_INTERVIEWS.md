# User interviews

I ran three short interviews with developers about how they use AI tools and what they think of an AI spend audit. I met **Yuvraj Tiwari** and **Updesh Chaudhary** through a WhatsApp group with many developers. **Mayank Yadav** is a friend from college.

At the end there is a short summary of patterns and ideas they raised.

---

## Interview 1 — Yuvraj Tiwari

**Role:** Full stack developer

**Company stage:** Not stated (independent / project work)

**How I reached them:** WhatsApp developer group

**Direct quotes:**

> See I use ai to develop things. I am a full stack developer but I don't like to design my ui , I am more of a logic writing person.

> I use mainly claude and chatgpt for llms. I also use github copilot because it helps write code faster due to suggestions. I also use codex and cursor to do some work in my projects.

> L like the idea of this audit report ai as It will save a lot of time. See for people like us it takes us a lot of time to decide which ai to use, what plans to buy. We have to check multiple sites to do this ,this project do it for us.

> A idea that i will recommend is to add a chat bot to allow people to chat with bot if they have any confusion in the report.

**Most surprising thing they said:** He was very clear that comparing plans across sites is real work — not a small annoyance — and that saving that time is the main win.

**What it changed about the design:** A chatbot on the report is on the backlog as a possible way to explain numbers without sending people back to Google.

---

## Interview 2 — Updesh Chaudhary

**Role:** Backend developer

**Company stage:** Not stated

**How I reached them:** Same WhatsApp developer group

**Direct quotes:**

> I use ai to learn new things. I also use them to create ppts, enhance my resume . It also helps me in creating an architecture of my project.

> I really like this idea of auditing because i use a lot of ai , i have subscription of claude and cursor.

> I think this project would not be that eneficial to us solo developers but for startups , it will be huge boost as it will allow them to easily manage their ai spending.

> I would like if the project will tie up with other ais and allow the user to buy the plan directly from auditing website, it will also allow you to make some profits.

**Most surprising thing they said:** He pushed back on solo devs versus startups — useful because it tells me who might feel the pain strongest first.

**What it changed about the design:** Positioning copy should speak to teams and shared spend, not only individual builders. Partnerships or “buy from here” flows are a later product conversation, not week one.

---

## Interview 3 — Mayank Yadav

**Role:** Flutter developer (early stage)

**Company stage:** Student / learning

**How I reached them:** Friend from college

**Direct quotes:**

> I have just started flutter development. Right Now i am still learning it so i think the best use of ai to teach me things related to flutter, to do some deep research on a topic.

> I dont have any subscription right now because i am a student , I do got some pro plans from chatgpt and gemini because of student privileges.

> For the project I will only say it would be pretty useful.

> I think we can improve the scale of this engine to audit not only ai credits but also other things and expenses happening in a tech startup.

**Most surprising thing they said:** Even without paid subs, he still called the audit “pretty useful” — so the idea is not only for heavy spenders.

**What it changed about the design:** Long term, “audit other startup tech spend” is a direction to remember; short term, the product stays focused on AI tool spend so it stays sharp.

---

## Short summary

**Patterns:** All three saw value in the core audit idea. Yuvraj focused on **time saved** and **less tab-hopping** to choose tools and plans. Updesh focused on **startups** and **centralising AI spend**. Mayank focused on **general usefulness** and **widening scope** someday.

**Ideas they gave:** Report **chatbot** (Yuvraj), **tie-ups / buy plans from the audit site** (Updesh), **broader startup expense auditing** (Mayank).

**Contradictions:** Updesh thought solo developers get less benefit than startups; Mayank is a student with light spend and still found it useful. Real interviews disagree a bit — that is normal and it keeps the notes honest.
